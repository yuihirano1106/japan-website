Tokyo is a vibrant megacity where ancient temples sit alongside futuristic skyscrapers, offering visitors an incredible blend of traditional and modern Japan. Must-see areas include the famous Shibuya crossing, the historic Asakusa district with Sensoji Temple, upscale shopping in Ginza, and the pop culture hub of Harajuku. The city boasts world-class dining from street food to Michelin-starred restaurants, plus an efficient train system that makes exploring easy. Don't miss Tokyo Disney Resort with its two unique parks - Disneyland and the stunning nautical-themed DisneySea - located just outside the city in Chiba Prefecture.

  • Asakusa(浅草) —temple Asakusa is built year of 628 and it's the oldest temple in tokyo. Asakusa is a fun old part of Tokyo. The main spot is Senso-ji Temple—you'll see a big red lantern at the entrance. Walk down the busy shopping street (Nakamise) for cheap snacks and souvenirs. You can also ride a rickshaw or see the Tokyo Skytree (the tall tower) nearby. Try crispy tempura or sweet melon bread while you're there! At night, the temple looks pretty with lights.
  • Tokyo Skytree(東京スカイツリー)—Tokyo Skytree is a broadcasting and observation tower located in Sumida City, Tokyo, Japan. Standing at 634 meters (2,080 feet) tall, it's the tallest structure in Japan and was the world's tallest tower when it opened in 2012.
    Address:1-chōme-1-2 Oshiage, Sumida City, Tokyo 131-0045, Japan
  • Ueno zoo(上野動物園) —Ueno zoo is open in 1882 and it's the first zoo ever in Japan. They have 300 species and 3000 animals in the zoo. also you can see beautiful cherry blossom in spring.
    Address: 9-83 Ueno Park, Taito-ku, Tokyo 110-8711, Japan
  • Tokyo tower(東京タワー)—Tokyo Tower is a 333-meter (1,092 ft) landmark in Minato, Tokyo, built in 1958. Inspired by the Eiffel Tower but lighter, it served as a broadcasting hub until Skytree surpassed it. It features two observation decks (150m and 250m) with stunning city views.
    Address: 4 Chome-2-8 Shibakoen, Minato City, Tokyo 105-0011, Japan
  • Odaiba(お台場)— Odaiba is a popular destination for Tokyo locals and international tourists alike, known for its shopping malls, dining, futuristic attractions, and museums, all easily accessible by foot.
    Address:1 Chome-4 Daiba, Minato City, Tokyo 135-0091, Japan
  • Shibuya,Sukuranburu-crossing(渋谷スクランブル交差点)—Shibuya Scramble Crossing is Tokyo's most famous intersection, where waves of people cross from all directions at once when the lights change. Surrounded by giant neon screens and skyscrapers, it feels like the heart of the city's energy.
  • Akihabara(秋葉原)—Akihabara is Tokyo's electric playground for anime, gaming, and tech lovers! Neon-lit streets are packed with shops selling manga, figurines, and cutting-edge electronics. Dive into multi-floor arcades, maid cafés, or otaku havens.

Osaka is Japan's third-largest city and the economic heart of the Kansai region, known as "Japan's kitchen" for its exceptional food culture including takoyaki (octopus balls), okonomiyaki (savory pancakes), and kushikatsu (fried skewers). The vibrant city features famous districts like Dotonbori with its neon lights and entertainment, historic Osaka Castle surrounded by beautiful gardens, and Universal Studios Japan theme park. With its friendly locals, lively atmosphere, and reputation as a merchant city, Osaka offers visitors a more down-to-earth experience compared to Tokyo, combining modern urban attractions with rich culinary traditions and Kansai culture.

  • Dotonbori(道頓堀)—The lively entertainment area of Dotonbori is Osaka's most famous tourist destination and renowned for its gaudy neon lights, extravagant signage, and the enormous variety of restaurants and bars.
  • Osaka Castle(大阪城)—Once the largest castle in Japan when originally built in the 1580s, this castle has an observation platform with city views and a history museum that uses holograms, 3D pictures and other technologies to describe the history of the castle and the period during which it was built.
    Address: 1-1 Osakajo, Chuo Ward, Osaka, 540-0002
  • Kaiyokan(海洋館) —See the wide range of habitats that make up the Pacific Rim on a tour of this fascinating aquarium, which takes you from one environment to another and lets you feel as though you're underwater.
    Address: 1 Chome-1-10 Kaigandori, Minato Ward, Osaka, 552-0022, Japan
  • Cupnoodles Museum(カップヌードルミュージアム) —Cupnoodles museum has an instant ramen workshop allowing visitors to make their own "fresh" instant noodles. Reservations must be made in advance to enjoy this feature at the museum. There is also a noodle factory where visitors can assemble their own personal Cup Noodles from pre-made ingredients for a small fee of 500 yen.
    Address:8-25 Masumicho, Ikeda, Osaka 563-0041, Japan
  • Universal Studios Japan(ユニバーサルスタジオ)—Universal Studios Japan is a fun theme park with exciting rides and shows. The park has attractions based on famous movies and popular characters. They also have special events during different seasons of the year. Visitors can enjoy many different activities and have a great time at the park.
    Address: 2 Chome-1-33 Sakurajima, Konohana Ward, Osaka, 554-0031, Japan
  • Tsutenkaku tower(通天閣)—Tsutenkaku is a famous tower in Osaka, Japan, standing 103 meters tall. Built in 1912 and rebuilt in 1956, it's located in the Shinsekai district and serves as a symbol of Osaka. The tower has an observation deck offering city views and is known for its colorful LED lighting at night. It's a popular tourist spot representing Osaka's retro charm and local culture.
    Address:1 Chome-18-6 Ebisuhigashi, Naniwa Ward, Osaka, 556-0002, Japan

Nagoya, Japan's fourth-largest city and the heart of the country's automotive industry, offers visitors a dynamic blend of modern innovation and historical heritage in central Honshu. Located strategically between Tokyo and Osaka, this major transportation hub provides easy access to the neighboring Gifu Prefecture, where traditional mountain villages like UNESCO World Heritage site Shirakawa-go and the historic town of Takayama showcase Japan's rural charm and centuries-old culture. Together, the Nagoya-Gifu region creates an ideal destination for travelers seeking both urban sophistication and authentic traditional experiences, all within convenient reach of Japan's bullet train network.

  • The Port of Nagoya Public Aquarium(名古屋港水族館) —The Port of Nagoya Public Aquarium is one of Japan's premier marine attractions, featuring around 500 species including orcas, dolphins, beluga whales, and penguins. The aquarium boasts Japan's largest outdoor tank and offers spectacular marine shows with detailed educational content, including popular dolphin performances that can seat 3,000 spectators. Located at Nagoya Port as part of the Garden Pier complex, it combines entertainment with marine education, making it a must-visit destination for families and marine life enthusiasts in the Nagoya area.
    Address: 1-3 Minatomachi, Minato Ward, Nagoya, Aichi 455-0033, Japan
  • Nagoya Castle(名古屋城)—Nagoya Castle, built in 1612 and reconstructed after WWII, is famous for its golden dolphins (kinshachi) that crown the main keep and serve as Nagoya's symbol. The castle features beautiful traditional architecture, the restored Honmaru Palace with painted sliding doors, and is surrounded by a scenic park in central Nagoya, making it one of the city's most important cultural landmarks.
    Address: 1-1 Honmaru, Naka Ward, Nagoya, Aichi 460-0031, Japan
  • Higashiyama Zoo and Botanical Gardens(東山動物園東山動物園) —Higashiyama Zoo and Botanical Gardens is one of Japan's most popular zoos, established in 1937 and covering about 600,000 m². The complex features over 550 animal species, Asia's largest plant collection with 7,000 species, and includes a zoo, botanical garden, and amusement park all in one location. Located in Nagoya and easily accessible by subway, it's famous for its 1,000 cherry trees that create spectacular spring blooms.
    Address: 3-70 Higashiyama-motomachi, Chikusa-ku, Nagoya, japan
  • Gero Onsen(下呂温泉)—Gero Onsen is one of Japan's three most famous hot springs, located in Gifu Prefecture and renowned for its healing waters with skin-beautifying properties. The charming mountain town offers traditional ryokan accommodations and public baths in a scenic setting. From Nagoya, Gero Onsen is easily accessible by express train in about 90-100 minutes on the JR Takayama Line with no transfers required, making it a perfect hot spring getaway destination for both day trips and overnight stays.
  • Shirakawa-go(白川郷)—Shirakawa-go is a UNESCO World Heritage mountain village in Gifu Prefecture, famous for its traditional gassho-zukuri farmhouses with steep thatched roofs that resemble praying hands. The village features 59 historic houses, some over 250 years old, creating a fairytale-like landscape that offers visitors a glimpse into traditional rural Japanese life.
  • Hida Great Limestone Cave(飛騨大鍾乳洞)—The Hida Great Limestone Cave is Japan's highest limestone cave at 900 meters elevation, located between Hida Takayama and Okuhida Onsen-go in Gifu Prefecture. Formed 250 million years ago, the cave features 800 meters of illuminated tunnels with impressive stalactites and rare helictite formations, maintaining a constant 12°C temperature year-round and creating a dreamlike underground experience for visitors.
    Address:1147 Nyukawacho Hiyomo, Takayama, Gifu 506-2256, Japan

Kyoto, Japan's former imperial capital, is renowned for its thousands of temples and shrines including the iconic golden Kinkaku-ji and Fushimi Inari's vermillion torii gates, along with traditional districts like Gion where geishas still practice their ancient arts. Nara, Japan's first permanent capital, is famous for its free-roaming sacred deer in Nara Park and the massive Todai-ji Temple housing one of Japan's largest bronze Buddha statues. Both ancient cities in the Kansai region offer visitors an authentic experience of traditional Japanese culture, Buddhism, and imperial heritage, making them essential destinations for understanding Japan's rich historical legacy.

  • Nara Park(奈良公園)—Nara Park is a 660-hectare public park famous for its 1,200 free-roaming sacred deer that bow politely when fed special deer crackers by visitors. The park houses important cultural sites including Todai-ji Temple with its massive bronze Buddha and Kasuga Taisha shrine. Established in 1880, it's one of Japan's most unique attractions where friendly deer and ancient temples coexist harmoniously.
  • Horyu-ji(法隆寺)—Horyu-ji is Japan's oldest Buddhist temple, built by Prince Shotoku in 607 AD and home to the world's oldest surviving wooden structures. Designated as Japan's first UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1993, the temple features 48 monuments that showcase the adaptation of Chinese Buddhist architecture into a distinctly Japanese style. Located in Ikaruga, Nara Prefecture, Horyu-ji serves as a treasure house of Buddhist culture and represents the profound legacy of early Buddhism in Japan.
    Address:1-1 Horyuji Sannai Ikaruga-cho, Ikoma-gun,Nara,Japan
  • Todai-ji(東大寺) —Todai-ji is one of Japan's largest and most significant temples, famous for housing the massive 15-meter-tall bronze Daibutsu (Great Buddha) statue. Built in the 8th century, the temple's main hall is one of the world's largest wooden buildings and serves as the head temple of Kegon Buddhism. Located in Nara Park among free-roaming deer, Todai-ji is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of Japan's most iconic religious landmarks.
    Address:406-1 Zoushi-cho Nara-shi Nara-ken 630-8587
  • Kyomizu-dera(清水寺)—Kiyomizu-dera is a historic Buddhist temple established in 778, famous for its wooden stage that juts out 13 meters above the hillside without using a single nail. The temple's name means "pure water" and refers to the waterfall within the complex, while the stage offers spectacular views of cherry and maple trees that create stunning seasonal colors. Located in Kyoto's Higashiyama district and designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1994, it's one of Japan's most iconic temples with over 1,200 years of history.
    Address:1 Chome-294 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto, 605-0862, Japan
  • Fushimi Inari Taisha(伏見稲荷神社)—Fushimi Inari Taisha is a Shinto shrine founded in 711, famous for its approximately 10,000 vermillion torii gates that create stunning tunnels leading up Mount Inari. Dedicated to Inari, the god of rice and agriculture, the shrine features fox statues throughout as foxes are considered Inari's messengers. The torii gates are donations from individuals and companies, making this iconic shrine with its hiking trails and panoramic views one of Japan's most photographed and visited religious sites.
    Address:68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi Ward, Kyoto, 612-0882, Japan
  • Kinkaku-ji(金閣寺)—Kinkaku-ji, known as the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen temple in northern Kyoto with its top two floors completely covered in gold leaf. Originally built as a retirement residence for Shogun Ashikaga Yoshimitsu in the 14th century and converted to a temple after his death, it was reconstructed in 1955 after being burned down in 1950. Famous for its stunning reflection in the adjoining mirror pond, Kinkaku-ji is perhaps the most widely-recognized and iconic image of Kyoto.
    Address:1 Kinkakujicho, Kita Ward, Kyoto, 603-8361, Japan
